Who Am I?

I’m a third year software engineering student at Addis Ababa University, a painter and an artist with a great focus on nature and a passion for learning new things as well as experiences.

When I was first grader, I met some one (who was actually a friend of my father) who was a great painter. I was astonished and he taught me some basic sketching. I used to spend my weekends sitting right to him while he was painting.

King of kings

Drawn in oct, 2020 GC, "Asiael" is also one of the drawing I love the most. Asiael( እስያኤል), who was an Aethiopian king around 1500 BC, is like a real superman in the history of mankind. .He was a wise, brave, smart and strong king. He was so legendary and he lived for around 400 years time period, ruling just about the whole world. In his early teenages, unlike son of a king, he used to spend his like in the jungle, learning from nature, which was a direct reflection of God's wisdom, and experimenting. His empire encompasses todays Asia( which was named after him), most of Europe as well as India. In the above painting I try to represent this legendary king in his young age. During his teenage, unlike son of a king, he used to live in the forest, learning from nature.

Saint Lalibela

Drawn in sep, 2020 GC, "Saint Lalibela" is a mixture of religious and secular drawing. King Lalibela (which is also a saint) ruled Ethiopia in the beginning of the 13 th century. He was so humble and smart as well as so religious. Even if his step sister and brother plotted to kill him during his childhood, he even didn't try to avenge them instead he fled to Jerusalem and stayed their till his return. The dar-cloudy-sky of background in the painting represents how much sufferance he had been through.

Ideas I Live By

1. Believe in Yourself , but Be Aware of Your Limitations

The first step to accomplishing all your goals and making your dreams come true starts with this simple realization that you are human: You are not perfect and you can’t do everything alone.

2. De-clutter and Simplify

You have a thousand different things screaming for attention: You have to tidy up the kids’ room again; you have to do the dishes and laundry; and the never-ending household chores are waiting. You have to organize your calendar and make room for more appointments; make time to socialise; help the kids with homework; and make a gazillion school runs. Let’s get one thing straight—you cannot accomplish anything unless you get yourself some of the clarity that comes from creating space in your life, in your relationships and your environment.

You need to reduce, cut back, simplify—Only then will you stop the feeling of being overwhelmed and rushed.

4. Keep Things in Perspective

I admit there will be times when nothing will go your way, and you will find yourself fighting battles, fixing problems and minimizing damage all day long.

Don’t sweat the small stuff. Have an open mind.
